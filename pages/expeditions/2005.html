The Story

Finding life with a robot

By 2005, the team had spent two field seasons getting Zoë to reliably drive herself. Now the question was whether she could do science.

The fluorescence imager — essentially a camera that illuminates the ground with ultraviolet light and photographs what glows back — was Zoë's primary life-detection tool. Living organisms fluoresce under UV in characteristic ways; rocks and mineral soil generally do not. As Zoë drove, she periodically stopped, deployed the instrument toward the ground, fired the UV source, and captured the result.
